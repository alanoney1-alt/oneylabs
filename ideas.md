## Approach A: Industrial Brutalism + Warm Amber

**Design Movement:** Neo-Brutalism with Southern warmth — raw structure softened by human touch

**Core Principles:**
1. Stark typographic hierarchy — headlines dominate, no decoration needed
2. Exposed grid lines and borders as design elements (not hidden)
3. Warm amber/orange accent against near-black backgrounds signals "builder energy"
4. Content-first: every pixel serves information, no decorative fluff

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: `#0D0D0D` near-black (not pure black — has warmth)
- Primary accent: `#F59E0B` amber — signals energy, craft, Florida warmth
- Secondary: `#1A1A1A` card surfaces
- Text: `#F5F0E8` warm off-white
- Muted: `#6B6B6B` for secondary text
- Intent: Feels like a workshop — serious tools, serious builder

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric two-column layouts with heavy left anchoring
- Oversized section numbers (01, 02, 03) as structural dividers
- Full-bleed dark sections alternating with tight card grids
- Horizontal rule lines as section separators (not whitespace alone)

**Signature Elements:**
1. Bold section counters in amber (01 / 02 / 03) — like a technical manual
2. Thick amber left-border cards for service tiers
3. Monospaced type for prices and data points (feels like code output)

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover states reveal amber underlines on links
- Cards lift with subtle box-shadow on hover
- CTA buttons use fill-from-left animation on hover

**Animation:**
- Entrance: Elements slide in from left (not fade — directional, purposeful)
- Scroll-triggered counter animations for stats
- No looping animations — everything is triggered by user action

**Typography System:**
- Display: `Syne` (bold, geometric, modern) for headlines
- Body: `IBM Plex Sans` (technical, readable, trustworthy)
- Mono: `IBM Plex Mono` for prices and code snippets
- Scale: 72px hero → 48px section → 32px subsection → 16px body

## Approach B: Clean Editorial with Electric Teal

**Design Movement:** Modern editorial — think Bloomberg Businessweek meets a local tech startup

**Core Principles:**
1. Magazine-style typography with editorial column layouts
2. High contrast — deep navy background, electric teal accent
3. Data visualization as design: numbers and stats are visual heroes
4. White space is generous but purposeful — not minimal for minimalism's sake

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: `#0A0F1E` deep navy (professional, trustworthy, tech)
- Primary: `#00D4AA` electric teal — signals AI, innovation, forward-thinking
- Cards: `#0F1829` slightly lighter navy
- Text: `#E8EDF5` cool white
- Muted: `#5A6A8A`
- Intent: Feels like a serious tech company that also happens to be approachable

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Editorial column grid: 12-column with deliberate column-spanning
- Pull quotes and callout boxes break the reading flow intentionally
- Sticky sidebar navigation on long service pages
- Horizontal scrolling card carousels for pricing tiers

**Signature Elements:**
1. Teal gradient text on key phrases ("AI for YOUR business")
2. Animated teal border that draws itself on scroll
3. Stats displayed in large editorial callout format

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Smooth page transitions (slide left/right between pages)
- Teal glow on interactive elements
- Parallax depth on hero section

**Animation:**
- Hero: Typewriter effect on headline
- Stats: Count-up animation on scroll
- Cards: Stagger reveal from bottom

**Typography System:**
- Display: `Clash Display` or `Bricolage Grotesque` — editorial, distinctive
- Body: `Plus Jakarta Sans` — modern, clean, readable
- Scale: 80px hero → 52px section → 36px subsection → 17px body

## Approach C: Raw Craft + Warm Dark (SELECTED)

**Design Movement:** Artisan Tech — the aesthetic of a master craftsman's workshop applied to digital

**Core Principles:**
1. Dark-first with warm undertones — not cold tech blue, but warm charcoal and cream
2. Typography as the primary design element — bold, confident, unapologetic
3. Proof over polish — real numbers, real product screenshots, real person
4. Local identity woven throughout — South Orlando isn't a footnote, it's the brand

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: `#111111` warm near-black
- Surface: `#1A1A1A` card backgrounds
- Primary accent: `#E8FF47` electric lime-yellow — unexpected, energetic, memorable
- Secondary accent: `#FF6B35` warm orange — Florida heat, builder energy
- Text: `#F0EDE6` warm cream (not pure white — has texture)
- Muted: `#888880` warm gray
- Intent: The lime-yellow is the "aha" moment — it signals speed, AI, and disruption without being corporate blue

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Staggered asymmetric layouts — no two sections use the same column structure
- Full-width text sections alternate with contained card grids
- Oversized typography that bleeds off-screen on mobile (intentional)
- Diagonal section dividers (CSS clip-path) to create visual momentum

**Signature Elements:**
1. Electric lime-yellow highlight bars behind key phrases
2. Oversized decorative quotation marks for testimonials
3. "Builder badge" — a small stamp/seal motif near the hero (handcrafted feel)

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover on service cards reveals a lime-yellow left border
- CTA buttons: solid lime-yellow with dark text (high contrast, impossible to miss)
- Navigation: minimal top bar that becomes sticky with a blur backdrop

**Animation:**
- Hero headline: words appear one at a time with slight upward motion
- Section entrance: content slides up from 20px below (subtle, not dramatic)
- Pricing cards: slight scale-up on hover (1.02x)
- No autoplay animations — all triggered by scroll or interaction

**Typography System:**
- Display: `Bebas Neue` — bold, condensed, builder energy (used ONLY for hero/section titles)
- Heading: `Manrope` — geometric, modern, readable (used for subheadings and nav)
- Body: `Lora` — serif, warm, trustworthy (used for body copy — signals "real person")
- Mono: `JetBrains Mono` — for prices, stats, technical callouts
- Scale: 96px hero → 56px section → 36px subsection → 18px body
